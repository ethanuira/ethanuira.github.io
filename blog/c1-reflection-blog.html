DBC

I'm not too sure on how to word my reviews, reflections, impressions and overall thoughts on this so this is most likely just gonna come out as a stream of thoughts so if it feels/seems disconjointed then I'm sorry. I'm also not the best at spelling in english so just keep that in mind too. I can assure you that my code is much tidier ;)

With that out of the way lets begin with my thoughts on Shereef's Fireside Chat.
I feel like what Shereef describes the DBC course as a very student-friendly course. He makes sure to take time out to talk to all students and try and cover all bases while keeping the talk realistic and letting the students know that it may not be easy for them, maybe someone might be ahead of you, maybe someone might be behind you, which I really appreciate. He also gives good adviced and tips that he uses to all the students. He uses good analogies to translate what it feels like being in a DBC class. I feel the same kind of energy, environment and working ethics from the EDA group and it comforts me a lot.

I enjoyed learning the roots of the idea of DBC and hearing why Shereef first taught someone coding and how things have changed and evolved and how he's adapted the course around that and how he devoted everything towards the idea of DBC.

I'm personally excited to be a part of this community more so than the teachers have made me feel like I already am. I feel nervous just because I feel like when I get to a classroom with other students I may worry that I am underperforming compared to my peers.

11th of September 2018
